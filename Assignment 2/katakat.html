Mughaz Plate

 Mughaz Plate, also known as Katakut, is a popular street food dish in South Asian cuisine, particularly in Pakistan. It features a flavorful combination of grilled or stir-fried goat or lamb brain (mughaz) mixed with spices, herbs, and served with a side of naan bread or paratha. This unique and indulgent dish is a favorite among adventurous food enthusiasts.
 To prepare Mughaz Plate or Katakut, follow this simple recipe:
 Start by cleaning the goat or lamb brain thoroughly and removing any excess membranes. Then, chop the brain into small, bite-sized pieces.
 Next, in a pan or skillet, heat oil or ghee over medium heat. Add finely chopped onions and saut√© them until they turn golden brown, giving the dish a caramelized flavor.
 Once the onions are browned, add ginger-garlic paste and cook for a minute until the raw aroma fades away.
 Now, it's time to add the chopped brain pieces to the pan. Stir-fry them for a few minutes until they are cooked through and turn slightly golden.
 To enhance the flavor, add a blend of aromatic spices such as turmeric, red chili powder, cumin, coriander, and salt according to your taste preferences. Mix well to coat the brain pieces evenly with the spices.
 Continue cooking for a few more minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together. Add a splash of water if needed to maintain a moist consistency.
 Finally, garnish the Mughaz Plate or Katakut with fresh coriander leaves and squeeze some lemon juice over the dish to add a tangy twist.
 Serve the Mughaz Plate or Katakut hot, alongside naan bread or paratha. It is often enjoyed as a street food snack or as a unique addition to a special meal.
 Mughaz Plate or Katakut offers a unique culinary experience, showcasing the tender and delicate flavor of goat or lamb brain combined with aromatic spices. It is a dish that brings together savory and tangy flavors, perfect for those seeking a distinctive taste adventure.
